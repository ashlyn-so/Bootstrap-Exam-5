IBM ThinkPad

Source: Les Pounder, File:IBM ThinkPad 380z (1).jpg, 10 July 2010, commons.wikimedia.org
(https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:IBM_ ThinkPad_380z_%281%29.jpg)

The Thinkpad was originally made by IBM and introduced in 1992. The laptop is known for their nice keyboard quality on a laptop. The Thinkpad was the first laptop to use a pointing stick for cursor movement. The first IBM thinkpads were the IBM 700 and IBM 700C. They were designed to be convenient to use and carry for business. The thinkpad 700 comes in a monochrome screen but has a color system that offers a pre-installed model with a capacity of 1200MB. Customers had the option to upgrade all system models to perform better.


The first thinkpads had a 25 MHz Intel processor with 4-16MB. The display for the thinkpad 700 model was 9.5in 640x480, and the thinkpad 700C model was 10.4in 640x480. The 700 Model weighs 6.5 pounds and costs $2,750 - $2,950. The 700C model weighs 7.6 pounds and costs $4,350.


The thinkpad was often praised and was known as the “butterfly” keyboard because of its unique design. The internal keyboard is separated into two halves. When the laptop's lid is opened, the halves swivel together to create an large keyboard that overhangs the sides of the case.
