Toshiba Libretto

Source: Dieter Bohn, Status Symbols: Toshiba Libretto - The Verge, 2 August 2013, theverge.com (https://www.theverge.com/2013/8/2/4566062/status-symbols-toshiba-libretto)

The Libretto is very small as it is only 210mm x 115mm x 34mm and is super lightweight at 840g. It includes a 65,536 6.1-inch TFT color LCD, an 88-key keyboard, a newer pointing device, a secondary battery, and a super-thin disk drive. It was by far the smallest laptop commercially at the time, and It offered a high performance and performed like a normal laptop despite its size. The device made it able to easily use it for portable work.


The Libretto CPU was equivalent to a 75 MHz DX4 processor and came with 8MB of memory. It has a Mass storage of 2.5-inch 270MB HDD with the worlds first 8.45 mm profile. It gave 2 to 3 hours of usage. Using an additional battery pack gave 6 hours of usage.


The original Libretto line was discontinued in Europe and the U.S. in 1999, but the production continued in Japan with the SS, FF and then the L series until 2002. The first L series Libretto was released only in Japan on 18 May 2001 and the last on 24 Apri 2002. All Librettos and stopped in 2002 until 2005.
